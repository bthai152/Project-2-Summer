Messerschmitt Bf 109

The Messerschmitt is a fighter plane that was used in World War 2. It was manufactured by a company named Dayerische Flugzeugwerke and was design by a duo. Their names were Willy Messerschmitt and Robert Lusser. This plane was the backbone of the Luftwaffe's fighter force The plane saw operational service in 1937 during the Spanish Civil War. Dubbed as the most advanced fighters of the ear the Bf 109 along with its experienced pliots proved to be a formative fighting force.

  • Role:Fighter
  • Manufacturer:Bayerische Flugzeugwerke(BFW),Messerschmitt AG
  • Designer: Willy Messerschmitt, Robert Lusser
  • First flight: May 29,1935
  • Introduction:Febuary 1937
  • Retired:May 9 1945 (Lufwaffe),December 27, 1965 (Spanish Air Force)
  • Primary users: Luftwaffe, Hungarian Air force, Aeronautica Nazionale Repunnlicana, Royal Romanian Air Force
  • Number built: 33,984, +239 HA-112, +603 Avia S-119
  • Variants: Avia S-99/S-199, Hispano Aviacion Ha 1112

Design of the Messerschmitt Bf 109

The Bf 109 had a all-metal monocoque construction, a closed canopy, and retractable landing gear. Its engine was a liquid cooled, inverted-V12 aero engine.
